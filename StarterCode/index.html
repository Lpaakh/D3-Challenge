Possible Correlations Discovered Between Smoking & Age

One can observe from the above plot that many smokers in the United States are between the ages of 33 and 43 years old. The factors influencing this 10 year age range could be attributed to multitude of differing generational characteristics, socialization factors, etc.

The highest prevalence of smokers seems to lie in the southern half of the United States with West Virginia, Kentucky, Arkansas, Tennessee and Louisiana topping the scatter plot (in that order).

The lowest prevalence of smokers appears to be in Utah, California, Hawaii, New York, Texas, and Maryland. Utah is by far the lowest with the only state being under 10 and the next lowest, California coming in at about 13.
